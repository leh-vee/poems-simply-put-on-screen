Like a blocked stenographer
poised over her keys, we wait
for the mayor’s final word on
Power Punchlines : A Decree.
Funny, we wrote it off as a joke
ages ago, how it should look
great if the city were festooned
by pairs of everyone's old shoes.
News is there’s been a surge
of hung sneakers, she says:
if heaven were a sky blue attic,
politely demanding a retraction. 
The New Balances come out
of a fog—we’re the dream’s poet
laureate after all. There we go,
on our way home: the most
unbelievable lives we’ll never
own, dangling just out of reach.
